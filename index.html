PALAWAN

Palawan is a province located in the western part of the Philippines, consisting of numerous islands surrounded by crystal-clear waters, vibrant coral reefs, and breathtaking limestone cliffs. Known for its stunning natural beauty and biodiversity, Palawan has been named one of the best islands in the world by various travel publications. It is home to several UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including the Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park and the Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park, both of which offer visitors a unique and unforgettable experience. Palawan is also a cultural melting pot, with its diverse population of indigenous peoples, Spanish-influenced settlers, and migrants from other parts of the Philippines. Whether you're looking for adventure, relaxation, or cultural immersion, Palawan has something for everyone.

Palawan Islands

Welcome to Palawan, a tropical paradise that boasts of 1,769 breathtaking islands and islets, making it a must-visit destination for beach lovers. With a total coastline of 1,959 kilometers, Palawan offers an endless variety of beaches that cater to all types of travelers.


Whether you're looking for a secluded and serene beach getaway or an action-packed adventure in the water, Palawan has it all. One of the most popular beach destinations in Palawan is El Nido, with its towering limestone cliffs, crystal-clear waters, and white sandy beaches.


If you're an adrenaline junkie, then Sabang Beach is the place for you. Here, you can experience the thrill of spelunking in the underground river, one of the world's seven natural wonders.


For those who prefer a more laid-back atmosphere, there's San Vicente & Port Barton Beach, a serene and tranquil beach with a small fishing village nearby. Here, you can relax on the beach, go snorkeling, or island hopping to explore the nearby islands.


With a land area of approximately 17,030 square kilometers, Palawan offers a diverse range of attractions that are sure to make your visit an unforgettable one. From its stunning beaches, Palawan has something for everyone. So come and discover the beauty of Palawan, and make memories that will last a lifetime!

Palawan Foods

Welcome to Palawan, a food lover's paradise! If you enjoy discovering new cuisines and trying local delicacies, then you are in for a treat. Palawan offers a diverse range of specialty dishes that are sure to tantalize your taste buds.


For those with a sweet tooth, make sure to try our famous Kakanin, a type of Filipino rice cake made from glutinous rice and coconut milk, topped with a variety of toppings such as grated coconut and caramel sauce. Another popular dessert is the Binagol, a sweet treat made from grated taro root mixed with coconut milk and sugar, then wrapped in banana leaves and steamed.


If you're looking for something more adventurous, be sure to try the Tamilok, a dish made from woodworms found in the mangrove forests of Palawan. It may sound unusual, but it is a local delicacy that many tourists rave about.


For those who prefer more familiar dishes, don't worry, Palawan also offers a wide range of seafood options, such as grilled tuna and lobster, as well as traditional Filipino dishes like Adobo and Sinigang.


No matter what your taste buds crave, Palawan's cuisine has something to offer. So come and experience the flavors of Palawan and discover why it is a must-visit destination for food lovers!

PALAWAN DESTINATIONS

EL NIDO

El Nido is a paradise located in the northernmost part of the Palawan province in the Philippines. It is known for its stunning limestone cliffs that tower over crystal-clear blue waters, white sandy beaches, elaborate coral reefs, and diverse marine life. El Nido is a top tourist destination in the Philippines, attracting visitors from all over the world. It is composed of several islands and islets, each offering unique experiences for visitors. Tourists can go island hopping, snorkeling, diving, kayaking, and even climb the towering cliffs to get the best panoramic view of the entire paradise. El Nido is truly a must-visit destination for travelers who are looking for a peaceful retreat and want to experience the natural beauty of the Philippines.

Sabang

Sabang is a natural wonder located on the west coast of Palawan in the Philippines. This small town is famous for its stunning UNESCO World Heritage site, the Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park, which features an awe-inspiring underground river that winds through caverns and rock formations. The underground river, which is also known as the "Second Wonder of the World," is a natural wonder that stretches for 8.2 kilometers and is home to several species of bats, marine creatures, and endemic flora and fauna. Sabang also offers visitors the opportunity to enjoy the stunning beauty of the surrounding nature, including lush forests, white sand beaches, and crystal-clear waters. Tourists can also visit nearby waterfalls, explore mangrove forests, hike the Sabang mountain range, and discover the unique culture and traditions of the local communities. Visiting Sabang is truly a once-in-a-lifetime experience that allows visitors to connect with nature and appreciate the natural beauty of the Philippines.

PORT BARTON & SAN VICENTE

Port Barton and San Vicente are two hidden gems situated in the northwestern part of Palawan in the Philippines. Port Barton is a small and quiet fishing village that features pristine beaches, turquoise waters, and lush forests. It is an ideal destination for travelers who want to escape the crowds and experience the natural beauty of Palawan. Port Barton offers visitors the opportunity to go island hopping, snorkeling, diving, and kayaking to discover the rich marine life and stunning coral reefs. San Vicente, on the other hand, is a more developed and bustling town that boasts a 14-kilometer long stretch of secluded beaches known as Long Beach. It is one of the longest white sand beaches in the Philippines and is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and beach activities. San Vicente is also home to several waterfalls, hot springs, and unique landscapes that are worth visiting. Port Barton and San Vicente offer visitors a taste of authentic Palawanese experience, where travelers can unwind and immerse themselves in the natural tropical paradise.

PALAWAN DELICACY

Palawan, known as the last ecological frontier of the Philippines, boasts not just pristine beaches and natural wonders but also a diverse range of delicious foods. Visitors can easily find a variety of tasty dishes that highlight the local cuisine and culture. Palawan cuisine is mainly infused with an abundance of fresh seafood, tropical fruits, vegetables, and coconut-based ingredients. Some of the must-try foods in Palawan include seafood delicacies such as grilled or fried squid, shrimps, and fish; kinilaw – a type of ceviche made with fish or seafood marinated in vinegar or calamansi juice; Tamilok – a raw woodworm delicacy best enjoyed with vinegar, chili, and onions; and Nido soup – made from the nest of the swiftlet bird, which is known for its highly nutritional value. Palawan cuisines also offer numerous dishes that incorporate local herbs and spices such as chaolong and crocodile sisig. For dessert, visitors can try the famous Bakers hill hopia. With its diverse and colorful cuisine, Palawan is a food lover's paradise that offers a unique culinary experience that is not to be missed.
